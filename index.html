WHAT ARE ANDROID STUDY JAMS?

Android Study Jams are community-organized study groups for people to learn how to build Android apps in the Kotlin programming language, using curriculum provided by Google. There are two tracks available:

  • New to Programming Track - for people who are new to programming, follows the Android Basics in Kotlin course.
  • Prior Programming Experience Track - for people who already have programming experience, follows the Android Kotlin Fundamentals course. Also includes a pre-work section using Kotlin Koans from JetBrains for those who are new to Kotlin.

Choose the track that would be most relevant for your community, and decide on pacing and number of sessions that the study group will meet for.

To host Android Study Jams for your chapter, you'll find numerous resources in the Android Study Jams content bundle which includes an organizer guide, sample attendee slides, presenter tips, and marketing templates you can customize. 

ORGANIZER CHECKLIST

Start here

  • Read the Android Study Jams Organizer Guide to become familiar with the program details.
  • Decide on which track you will host: New to Programming Track or Prior Programming Experience Track.
  • Choose how far in the course curriculum you want to go. Will you complete the first few pathways in the course or all pathways available? The description of each pathway is included in the organizer guide. Completing more pathways will enable attendees to create more fully featured Android apps.
  • Decide how many Android Study Jams sessions you will hold (based on time estimates of each pathway in the organizer guide).
  • Decide if you will host Android Study Jams as virtual or in-person events. 
    • Virtual event: Review this guidance on Hosting Virtual Events and determine dates/times of the sessions.
    • In-person event: Find a venue and determine the dates/times of the sessions. 
  • (Optional) Review the train-the-trainer recording where we spoke about the content and resources available for your team to organize these Android Study Jams for your community.

Before a session

  • Create the event on DSC/GDG event platform: Check out the instructions to create the event in the section below.

  • Prepare content: In preparation for the event, youll want to familiarize yourself and any co-speakers with the slides.

  • Plan agenda: Its important to plan an agenda and share it with co-organizers and attendees so everyone is on the same page. Check out the organizer guide for some sample agendas.

  • Send pre-event message: Before the event, its always good to send a pre-event message to explain any logistics and get your community excited for the event. You can do so using the event platform: Your Chapter Dashboard -> Emails -> Audience: People with event RSVP -> Event: Your Android Study Jams event. Example communications: new to programming, prior programming experience, social media.

During a session

  • Check-in Attendees: Please make sure you check in your attendees during the session through the DSC/GDG event platform. You can go to your Chapter Dashboard -> Events -> Select your Android Study Jam event -> Check in attendees.

  • Have fun: You bring the energy to the session, so be sure to come energized and ready to have some fun!

  • Present deck & content demos: Present the materials that you prepared.

  • Answer questions: While everyone is working on pathways, make sure everyone knows youre available for questions. Feel free to check on people and ask them how things are going, rather than wait for people to come to you.

  • Encourage attendees: The attendees worked hard and learned a lot. At the end of a session, acknowledge their progress, pointing out milestones and achievements to encourage them along the way!

After a session

  • [Required] Submit post-event survey: Fill out the post-event survey from the event platform to record how the event went. 

    • [Optional] If you have any specific feedback on the Android Study Jams content, fill out this form here! 

  • Share slides with attendees: Attendees will want access to your deck after the session, so make sure to make a copy and send it out afterwards, or just post a short link at the end of the event.

  • Gauge interest in more events: This can act as a springboard for more Android Study Jam sessions or general Android meetups in your community -- dont lose momentum! Make sure people know where to look if they want to learn more.

CREATE THE EVENT ON DSC/GDG EVENT PLATFORM

  • Locate your chapter from within the Dashboard and select Events from the left sidebar.
  • Click New Event and select the appropriate event type based on the track and whether the event is in-person or virtual.
    • Android Study Jams: New to Programming - Virtual
    • Android Study Jams: New to Programming - In person
    • Android Study Jams: Prior Programming Experience - Virtual
    • Android Study Jams: Prior Programming Experience - In person
  • Fill out the appropriate fields:  
    • Title, Short Description, Event Description
      • Please mention the facilitator's name in the event description
      • Topics - select Android
      • For event description, describe what the attendees will learn in that session (e.g. topics covered), what equipment they will need to bring, and any prerequisites. You can see our communication templates for ideas: new to programming or prior programming experience
    • Schedule & Agenda
  • Once you create the event, youll get an event link which you can start using to promote the event and people can start to RSVP.
  • Start sharing and promoting your event page through your community and social media, as detailed in the next section.

CERTIFICATE OF COMPLETION TEMPLATE

There is a certificate template you can use for attendees who successfully complete the Android Kotlin Fundamentals course as part of the Prior Programming Experience Track of Android Study Jams. This is exclusively available for Google Developer communities (such as WTM, GDG, and DSC members).

This template can be used by you, the community lead, to award members of your Google Developer community who complete all 10 pathways of the Android Kotlin Fundamentals course. This is optional, but a great way to recognize and celebrate the hard work of your members! 

1. Have each attendee email you a screenshot of their Google Developer Profile with their name and the 10 badges of the Android Kotlin Fundamentals course. Alternatively, you could also use a Google Form and have them upload their screenshots.

2. Fill in their name in the provided certificate template, certifying by you that they completed the course. Also fill in the rest of the details (location, date, community logo, and community name). Use an honor system to award these certificates, so make sure that they are participating and learning at your events!

Open in Google Slides

Note: a certificate is not available for the New to Programming Track because it relies on the Android Basics in Kotlin course and all units of that course have not been released yet.

FAQs

Who is the audience for Android Study Jams?

This program is for people who are new to Android development and want to learn the best practices for how to build Android apps in Kotlin. There are two different tracks based on whether someone has prior programming experience or not. As always, you have the flexibility to adapt the level, format, duration, and other aspects of the agenda based on the needs of your community. 

What are the prerequisites for participating?

  • For the New to Programming track, the prerequisites are basic computer skills and basic math skills.
  • For the Prior Programming Experience track, the prerequisites are prior programming experience in an object-oriented programming language, being familiar with how to use an IDE, and being familiar with using GitHub.

What equipment is needed?

Each student should have access to a computer with internet access. It is optional to have a physical Android device and USB cable (to connect to the computer). The computer should meet these system requirements, in order to install Android Studio for building Android apps.

Should attendees install Android Studio on their computers beforehand?

This decision is up to your preference.

Option 1: You can have attendees install Android Studio as part of the hands-on codelabs. There are instructions within the two tracks for students to install Android Studio. This will take up more time during your session, but will allow them to ask questions if they need help with the install and set up process.

Option 2: You could ask attendees to install Android Studio on their own before attending a session. Just be sure to let them know that this is expected of them prior to the session. These instructions may be useful.

How should attendees access the content?

At the start of the first session of Android Study Jams, direct attendees to go to g.co/android/studyjams. This will lead them to the landing page for Android Study Jams, where they will earn their exclusive Android Study Jams participant badge. They can add this to their Google Developer Profile. Then they can click the course under the appropriate track. These instructions are also located within the sample attendee slides for both tracks.

Future badges that attendees earn in the pathways will also get added to their profile. They can access their Google Developer Profile here.

Do I need to use the provided attendee slides?

It's not required for you to use the provided slides at your Android Study Jam sessions. They are simply provided as a bonus resource for you to use if you'd like. Make a copy and customize them (e.g. agenda) before using.

Do I need to create my own slides for future sessions?

If you want to create your own slides, there are tips in the Presenter Guide to help you. However, it's not required for you to create your own slides. If you want to have attendees dive right into the hands-on content during the sessions, that's a perfectly valid option as well. Feel free to adapt to what you think would work best for you and your community.

What are the hashtags for this event?

#AndroidStudyJams #DeveloperStudentClubs (if applicable) and your own community hashtag.

When should these events take place?

This content can be used anytime for your chapters.

Is this a global series or limited to specific regions?

It is global. We welcome all developer communities from around the world to participate and create their own events.

Can multiple communities in a city collaborate on an event?

Yes, absolutely!

Can we have #AndroidStudyJams as part of our conference or DevFest event?

Yes, you can use the content as part of any type of conference or event. For example, you could hold sessions 1 and 2 of a specific track at an event, and any participants interested in continuing with the curriculum can continue to meet for further sessions after the conference or event.
